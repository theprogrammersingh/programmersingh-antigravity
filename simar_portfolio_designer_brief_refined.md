# Portfolio Website UI/UX Brief for Designer

## Project Name
Personal Portfolio Website for **Simar Preet Singh**

---

## 1. Project Objective
Design a modern personal portfolio website that presents **Simar Preet Singh** as:
- an experienced **Software Engineer**
- a **front-end / full-stack developer** with strong product experience
- a **public speaker and workshop facilitator**
- a **community builder and organiser** in the North India developer ecosystem

The website should help with:
- personal branding
- speaking and conference opportunities
- recruiter and hiring visibility
- collaboration and consulting opportunities
- community credibility

---

## 2. Core Positioning
The website should communicate this identity clearly:

**Software Engineer, speaker, and community builder with 8+ years of experience building web products, delivering developer sessions, and growing local tech communities in Punjab and beyond.**

---

## 3. Target Audience
### Primary audiences
1. **Recruiters / Hiring Managers**
   - Want to quickly understand experience, skills, current role, and strengths.
2. **Conference Organisers / DevRel Teams**
   - Want to see speaking credibility, topics, past sessions, and community presence.
3. **Founders / Collaborators / Clients**
   - Want to understand what kind of products and platforms Simar can build.
4. **Developer Community Members / Students**
   - Want to explore workshops, talks, mentorship, and community contributions.

---

## 4. Design Direction
### Overall feel
- modern
- premium but approachable
- developer-first
- clean and structured
- high trust
- slightly bold personality, but not flashy

### Visual personality
The design should balance three identities:
1. **Engineer** – technical, reliable, sharp
2. **Speaker** – visible, energetic, public-facing
3. **Community Builder** – warm, human, accessible

### Design notes
- Avoid overly corporate resume-style design.
- Avoid cluttered card overload.
- Use strong typography and spacing.
- Use a layout that feels like a polished portfolio, not just a converted CV.
- Motion can be subtle, not distracting.
- The site should work well on mobile because many organisers and community members may open links from social media.

---

## 5. Recommended Website Structure
A **single-page website with section-based navigation** is recommended.

### Main sections
1. Hero
2. About / Profile
3. Career Highlights
4. Work Experience
5. Skills / Tech Stack
6. Featured Work
7. Speaking & Sessions
8. Community & Volunteering
9. Mentoring / Judging / Hackathons
10. Writing & Open Source
11. Education
12. Contact

### Optional later expansion
- Separate **Talks page**
- Separate **Projects page**
- Downloadable **Speaker Profile / Media Kit**
